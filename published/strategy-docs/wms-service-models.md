<div class="section-label">What's actually being sold — three distinct products inside the offer</div>

<p class="section-intro">Different commercial models lead with different products. Before choosing a model, be clear about which product is doing the work. The most common mistake is leading with Product B (the infrastructure) when buyers are buying Product A (the methodology) or Product C (the judgement).</p>

<div class="card-grid cols-3">
  <div class="card">
    <div class="card-num c-blue">PRODUCT A</div>
    <div class="card-title">The Methodology</div>
    <div class="card-body">
      The six-phase task library. Done_when criteria. SKILL seed files. The validated sequence of what to do and in what order, with observable completion conditions. <strong>This is the hard-won IP.</strong> It took engagements to produce and cannot be replicated without them.
      <br><br>
      <span class="cat-tag">Licenceable</span>&nbsp;
      <span class="cat-tag">Transferable</span>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="card">
    <div class="card-num c-teal">PRODUCT B</div>
    <div class="card-title">The Infrastructure</div>
    <div class="card-body">
      Spec A + Spec B + the template set. Replicable, inspectable, self-hosted. The architecture that makes the "no black box" answer credible. <strong>Low marginal cost to replicate, high setup value.</strong> A competitor can copy the spec; they can't copy the methodology it governs.
      <br><br>
      <span class="cat-tag">Open</span>&nbsp;
      <span class="cat-tag">Self-hosted</span>&nbsp;
      <span class="cat-tag">Inspectable</span>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="card">
    <div class="card-num c-amber">PRODUCT C</div>
    <div class="card-title">The Advisory Relationship</div>
    <div class="card-body">
      Mike and Peter's pattern recognition. Phase transition judgement. The ability to read a failing pilot before the client can and redirect it. <strong>Cannot be documented or replicated.</strong> This is what grows with every engagement and what the client is actually paying for when they extend.
      <br><br>
      <span class="cat-tag">Non-replicable</span>&nbsp;
      <span class="cat-tag">Compounds</span>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="task-card open">
  <div class="task-header">
    <div class="task-id">01</div>
    <div class="task-title">The Capability Transfer Engagement<br><span style="font-weight:300;font-size:11.5px;color:rgba(255,255,255,0.5)">Fixed scope · defined deliverables · clean handover · client owns everything</span></div>
    <div class="task-ws ws-pilot">Ready now</div>
    <div class="task-expand">▾</div>
  </div>
  <div class="task-body">
    <div>
      <div class="task-section-label">The pitch</div>
      <p>We run the engagement alongside your team for a defined period. At the end, you own a working WMS, a pre-populated task library configured for your context, and a SKILL library seeded with what we've learned. We are not required to operate it. You hire us because of what we transfer, not because of what we retain.</p>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:5px;flex-wrap:wrap;margin-top:8px;">
        <span class="side-tag side-wms">Methodology</span>
        <span class="side-tag side-tpl">Infrastructure</span>
        <span class="side-tag side-both">Advisory</span>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div>
      <div class="task-section-label">Structure &amp; revenue</div>
      <ul style="padding-left:16px;">
        <li><strong>Fee:</strong> Fixed project fee, scoped by phase (Phase 1–4 as a typical first engagement)</li>
        <li><strong>Duration:</strong> 12–20 weeks depending on org size and governance complexity</li>
        <li><strong>Deliverables:</strong> Working WMS, configured task library, SKILL seed, Week One guide, handover session</li>
        <li><strong>Ongoing:</strong> Optional advisory retainer at Phase 5+ (see Model 4)</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div>
      <div class="task-section-label">Who buys it</div>
      <p>Organisations with genuine internal capability who want to build it themselves but want the methodology to be right from the start. CTO or AI programme lead is the decision-maker. The "no black box, no dependency" positioning lands hardest here. Regulated industries: financial services, healthcare, public sector.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="task-note">
      <strong>Honest tension:</strong> This model is commercially correct but creates a ceiling. A client who genuinely achieves independence has less reason to engage beyond Phase 4. The answer is not to engineer dependency — it's to make the advisory retainer (Model 4) genuinely valuable enough that independent clients choose it anyway. The SKILL library review and methodology updates are what earn that.
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="task-card">
  <div class="task-header">
    <div class="task-id">02</div>
    <div class="task-title">The Accelerator Sprint<br><span style="font-weight:300;font-size:11.5px;color:rgba(255,255,255,0.5)">2–4 weeks · high intensity · jumpstart for teams that already have capability</span></div>
    <div class="task-ws ws-pilot">Ready now</div>
    <div class="task-expand">▾</div>
  </div>
  <div class="task-body">
    <div>
      <div class="task-section-label">The pitch</div>
      <p>For organisations that already have AI capability and don't need the full methodology transfer — they need the starting point to be right. We seed the WMS, configure the task library for their first pilot, train the internal lead, and leave. The client runs from week five onwards. Higher daily rate, lower total fee, faster time-to-value.</p>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:5px;flex-wrap:wrap;margin-top:8px;">
        <span class="side-tag side-wms">Methodology</span>
        <span class="side-tag side-tpl">Infrastructure</span>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div>
      <div class="task-section-label">Structure &amp; revenue</div>
      <ul style="padding-left:16px;">
        <li><strong>Fee:</strong> Fixed sprint fee, typically 60–70% of a full engagement</li>
        <li><strong>Duration:</strong> 2–4 weeks intensive, plus one check-in at week 8</li>
        <li><strong>Deliverables:</strong> WMS built and seeded, Phase 1–2 tasks loaded, internal lead trained, first pilot brief written</li>
        <li><strong>Ongoing:</strong> Follow-on engagements if Phase 3+ support is needed</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div>
      <div class="task-section-label">Who buys it</div>
      <p>Scale-ups and mid-market organisations with a strong internal technical lead who wants the methodology without the full consulting engagement. Also suits organisations that tried AI implementation without a framework and stalled — they don't need education, they need the right starting conditions.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="task-note">
      <strong>Honest tension:</strong> The Accelerator Sprint risks being under-scoped. A two-week sprint that seeds an incorrect task library or misconfigures the governance layer costs more to fix than it would have cost to do right the first time. The done_when criteria on the sprint deliverables need to be as rigorous as the client-facing task library itself.
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

      <p>The task library, SKILL seed, and spec documents are licenced assets with an annual update cycle. Clients pay to access the current version of the methodology and receive updates as it evolves. The WMS infrastructure is free to build from the spec; the content is the product. Closest to a recurring revenue model without a hosted platform.</p>
      <div style="display:flex;gap:5px;flex-wrap:wrap;margin-top:8px;">
        <span class="side-tag side-wms">Methodology</span>
      </div>
    </div>

      <div class="task-section-label">Structure &amp; revenue</div>
      <ul style="padding-left:16px;">
        <li><strong>Fee:</strong> Annual licence, tiered by organisation size</li>
        <li><strong>What's included:</strong> Current task library version, SKILL seed, spec docs, one orientation session per year</li>
        <li><strong>Updates:</strong> New task library phases, updated SKILL files, revised done_when criteria as methodology matures</li>
        <li><strong>Excludes:</strong> Advisory time, configuration support, phase transition support — these are add-ons</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div>
      <div class="task-section-label">Who buys it</div>
      <p>Organisations that completed a full engagement (Model 1) and want to stay current as the methodology evolves. The licence model is most viable once the task library has been validated across 4–5 engagements and the update cycle is credible.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="task-note">
      <strong>Honest tension:</strong> A licence with no consulting attached is only valuable if the methodology updates are meaningfully better than what the client could develop themselves. That credibility comes from running engagements. The licence model is a second-generation product — it becomes viable after Models 1 and 2 have generated enough validated patterns to make the update cycle genuinely worth paying for.

The risk is that it positions AI Value Worx as a technical implementation partner rather than a strategic methodology provider, which may compete with Models 1–4 for internal positioning.

<div class="section-label">How the models combine — the natural client journey</div>

<p class="section-intro">A client doesn't buy one model — they move through them.</p>

```
Prospect sees a case study or referral
│
▼
Model 02  Accelerator Sprint    ←── lower barrier, faster proof of value
     or
Model 01  Capability Transfer   ←── full engagement, enterprise clients
│
│  engagement completes, client is running independently
▼
Model 04  Embedded Advisor Retainer  ←── sold at project close, not separately
│
│  methodology matures across 4–6 engagements
▼
Model 03  Methodology Licence        ←── past clients, new clients with capability
    +
Model 05  Partner Programme          ←── consultancies wanting the methodology
│
│  client is building AI products, not just implementing AI
▼
Model 06  Technical Accelerator      ←── different buyer, different entry point
```

Models 01 and 04 are the engine — a fixed-scope engagement that closes cleanly and converts to an advisory retainer is the highest-value transaction in the set. Models 03, 05, and 06 are second-generation plays that become viable once the methodology has been validated across enough engagements to be taught and licenced credibly. **The mistake would be building Models 03, 05, or 06 before Models 01 and 04 are working reliably.**

<div class="section-label">The one strategic choice that determines which models to build first</div>

<div class="card-grid cols-2">
  <div class="card">
    <div class="card-num c-blue">PATH A</div>
    <div class="card-title">Services business</div>
    <div class="card-body">
      <p>Deepen the methodology through engagements. Build the SKILL library. Become the best practice authority.</p>
      <p>The methodology gets better with every engagement. The advisory relationship gets more valuable as the pattern library grows. Revenue comes from high-value engagements and retainers, not from licence fees or platform subscriptions.</p>
      <ul style="padding-left:16px;margin-top:8px;">
        <li>Start with Models 01 and 02</li>
        <li>Build Model 04 retainers from every engagement</li>
        <li>Add Model 05 partners when the methodology is proven</li>
        <li>Models 03 and 06 are optional extensions</li>
      </ul>
      <p style="margin-top:12px;"><strong>Constraint:</strong> Revenue scales with people. The ceiling is senior practitioner time.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="card">
    <div class="card-num c-teal">PATH B</div>
    <div class="card-title">Product business</div>
    <div class="card-body">
      <p>Codify the methodology as a licenceable product. Build recurring revenue. Scale without scaling headcount.</p>
      <p>The task library, SKILL seed, and spec documents become a product with an annual update cycle. Partners deliver it. The platform is the WMS. Revenue comes from licences, not from time.</p>
      <ul style="padding-left:16px;margin-top:8px;">
        <li>Start with Models 01 and 02 to validate content</li>
        <li>Build Model 03 as the recurring revenue mechanism</li>
        <li>Build Model 05 for delivery leverage</li>
        <li>Models 04 and 06 are optional add-ons</li>
      </ul>
      <p style="margin-top:12px;"><strong>Constraint:</strong> Requires product investment: versioning, certification, partner support. Not viable before 4–6 engagements have validated the content.</p>

<div class="section-label">The most useful immediate action</div>

<div class="banner banner-blue">
  <strong>Validate the methodology with the pilot client before designing the commercial model.</strong>
</div>

The pilot client who has already signed is the most valuable thing you have right now — not because of the revenue, but because they are the first real validation of whether the task library, done\_when criteria, and SKILL seed work the way the spec says they do. **Every assumption the commercial model rests on gets tested in that engagement.** The debrief after Phase 2 will tell you more about which commercial model is right than any planning document can. Run the engagement rigorously, capture everything in the SKILL library, and let the debrief inform the model choice. The documentation package you have now is strong enough to start selling Models 01 and 02 in parallel — but don't commit to Models 03, 05, or 06 until the pilot tells you whether the methodology is as good in practice as it is on paper.
